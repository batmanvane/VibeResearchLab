Animation Guide

Understanding what the simulation visualizes and how to read it

1. What the Animation Shows

The animation simulates 2 years of research activity comparing two labs:

  • Left — Traditional Lab: 1 PI + 3 PhDs working without AI assistance over 2 years
  • Right — Augmented Lab: The same team, now aided by AI tools — completing in ~2 weeks of simulated time

The research activity is visualized as a growing network of nodes and connections. Each node represents a research element (idea, method, result, publication) and edges show how they build on each other. The networks grow at different rates — the Augmented Lab's network is denser and more connected because the model predicts higher productivity.

Each simulation run is stochastic — events occur probabilistically, so the exact growth pattern varies between runs. The expected output factor converges to ~1.5x over time, not the 3-5x that vendors claim.

3. Network Visualization

3.1 Node Types

NodeMeaning
● White / largePI (Principal Investigator) — the center hub of the network
● Yellow / orange / greenPhD researchers — spawn new research directions
● BluePublication draft — research direction completed
● PurpleDerivation — mathematical progress
● Cyan / skyNumerical result — computational progress
● OrangeConsistency check — validation of results
● RedDead end — abandoned or failed direction
○ TransparentResearch directions in progress (not yet resolved)

3.2 Edges

Lines connect nodes to show dependencies — a new derivation building on a previous result, a publication draft connecting multiple numerical results, and so on. Denser connections mean more cumulative research progress.

3.3 Zoom Behavior

Both sides zoom out over time as the network grows. The Augmented Lab's network is larger, so it zooms out faster to keep everything visible. The relative zoom reflects the productivity gap — you can see the right side's network becoming visually busier faster.

4. Header Elements

4.1 Lab Labels

TRADITIONAL LAB and AUGMENTED LAB label each panel. Below each label:

  • Traditional: 1 PI + 3 PhDs · 2 years — indicates the team composition and real-world timespan
  • Augmented: 1 PI + 3 PhDs + AI · 2 weeks — same team with AI, compressed to weeks for visual alignment

4.2 Progress Bars

Below the lab labels, a small progress bar shows the cumulative number of events logged. The bar fills as events accumulate, giving a sense of ongoing activity in each lab.

Each event is counted and displayed as events: N below the bar.

4.3 PhD Color Legend

A small inline legend shows the color coding for each of the three PhD researchers (PhD 1, PhD 2, PhD 3). Their nodes appear in their respective colors throughout the network.

5. Output Factor

The output factor is the ratio of events logged by the Augmented Lab vs. the Traditional Lab. It is displayed between the two panels (or between sections on mobile).

  • The number scales based on the current productivity ratio
  • It turns green only when the ratio exceeds 1.5x — avoiding the false impression that small gains are significant
  • On mobile it is displayed larger (20px font) for visibility in the stacked layout

Expected Value: ~1.5x

The model predicts approximately 1.5x output factor — not 3-5x as vendors claim. This is grounded in published RCTs (Peng et al., Noy & Zhang) and independent studies (METR).

6. Productivity Graph (Desktop Only)

The graph in the center (desktop only) plots the productivity ratio over simulated time:

  • X-axis: Output ratio (1x to 3x)
  • Y-axis: Simulated time (0 → 2 weeks for Augmented, 0 → 2 years for Traditional)
  • Line: Tron-style glowing line that grows from bottom as time progresses — shows how the ratio evolves
  • Glow dot: The leading edge of the line shows the current ratio at the current time

On the left side of the graph, time is labeled in years (Traditional Lab). On the right side, it is labeled in weeks (Augmented Lab) — though they share the same vertical position, reflecting the different durations.

The graph is removed on mobile due to space constraints. The output factor still appears between sections.

7. Progress Trackers

At the bottom of each panel (or section on mobile), the progress tracker shows categorized research activity as filled cells. There are five rows:

RowColorMeaning
Publication draftGreenCompleted research directions that could become publications — the rarest, highest-value events
DerivationPurpleMathematical derivations or proofs — core research progress
Numerical resultBlueComputational or experimental results generated
Consistency checkOrangeValidation, debugging, and cross-checking of results — includes hallucination checking overhead
Dead endRedAbandoned research directions (approximately 33% of directions fail, per Open Science Collaboration 2015)

Each cell represents a logged event. When a row's bar fills, it wraps and clears — the number of wraps is included in the total count. The trackers for both labs can be compared side by side (or top/bottom on mobile) to see the difference in activity composition.

Note: The Augmented Lab's tracker shows more activity overall (reflecting the ~1.5x output factor) and relatively more consistency checks (orange) because AI hallucination checking adds overhead. It may also show slightly more dead ends due to AI-generated incorrect results that must be caught and discarded.

8. The Info Button

Click the i button in the top-right corner to open a slide-in panel with:

  • The mathematical model summary
  • Key empirical findings from published studies
  • Per-task speedup values and their sources
  • A link to the full Mathematical Model page

9. Running the Simulation

The animation runs automatically on page load. Each run is stochastic — randomness in event timing means no two runs look identical, just like real research labs.

The simulation completes in about 2 minutes of real time. To restart, simply refresh the page.
